Intro to Cyber Security

Cyber Security is now a global issue and why it is important for companies to invest in Cyber Security.

Cyber criminals are all around and they are waiting to find the next most vulnerable aspect of any business that they can attack. Among many other companies that fell victims to various cyber-attacks, my post was on EasyJet. It is obvious that this occurrence affected the prestige and the future business of the company. EasyJet is one of the world’s most successful low-cost airlines. Unfortunately, last two years, it became the victim of a cyber-attack in which data of 9 million of its customers was compromised. The cyber-attack, which saw the email addresses and travel details of millions of passengers being robbed—as well as the credit card details of some 2,000—was reportedly conducted by the very same group of Chinese hackers responsible for other attacks on a number of airlines in recent months (Mühlberg,2020). This has also led to a class-action lawsuit against the company which 10,000 of its customers have apparently joined. The lawsuit claims that the airline was in violation of the UK’s Data Protection Act of 2018. Easy Jet, has a further instantaneous judicial issue as a result of legal firm PGMBM, that has released a class-action-claim over £18 billion contingent debt, or equal to £2,000 per affected customer (Osborne,2020). Inadequate infrastructure on IT regarding the airline’s website was presumably the cause why the cyberpunks effortlessly retrieved the customers data. The major weakness was the exposure in the third-party java script served in the website (McGuire,2020). In agreement to this response from Matjaz; “EasyJet should have put enough security measures in place to prevent leakage of such personal information beforehand. Despite the incident being reported to the Information Commissioner’s Office on time, it took EasyJet four months to notify their customers about the breach (Wakefield, 2020). The Information Commissioner’s Office then advised that EasyJet customers should take cyber security steps against phishing (Wakefield, 2020). I am not surprised that PGMBM and other law firms are preparing class action to compensate EasyJet customers for such large personal data breach (Gound, 2020).” Also, from Rouda; This incident should serve as a valuable lesson for all companies, and they should immediately begin taking measures to ensure that they are in compliance with the GDPR.

Cyber Security Technologies.

For a secured system this post discusses Antivirus and Intrusion Prevention System as security technologies for an organization. Antivirus is a software package designed to detect, isolate, and remove malicious code (otherwise known as malware) from a computer system. Norton 360 has an anti-malware engine which uses machine learning, advanced heuristics, and a consistently updated malware directory to identify malware - from simple viruses and trojans to advanced malware like spyware, ransomware, and cryptojackers. Norton also has firewall, web protection, anti-phishing protection, VPN, Password Manager, device optimization, cloud backup among others. As contributed by Latif, IPS is a great network security and threat prevention tool, IPS systems examine the traffic flow and take actions against malicious software and identify the malicious activity. Difference between IDS and IPS is that IDS only detects the malicious activity whilst IPS can both detect and prevent. IPS lowers the chances of security incidents by allowing or denying specific traffic to the system as it sits behind the firewall. Multiple IPSeS can be used on a single network but IPS requires bandwidth to work with as it filters out the traffic, if a system does not have enough bandwidth, it can make the system slow and can cause a loss in network performance. Some cyber security advantages include Productivity enhancement, data protection, business reputation protection, assists remote working, regulation compliance, maintains trust and credibility among others. Disadvantages include; costly for businesses, complications, requirement of constant monitoring among others.
